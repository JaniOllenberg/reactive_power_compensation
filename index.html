The Project

This repository documents the evolution of a Power Factor (PF) Correction tool. Power factor is the ratio of Real Power (kW) to Apparent Power (kVA). When it's low, the system is inefficient, drawing more current than necessary and often incurring utility penalties.

Why it matters:

  • Current Reduction: Higher PF means lower total current for the same work.
  • Heat Loss: Lower current reduces $I^2R$ losses in cables and transformers.
  • Economics: Modern utilities charge for "Reactive Energy" (kVARh) if the PF drops below a threshold (usually 0.95).
